UK Online Safety Act:

The UK Online Safety Act is a new law that requires internet platforms to monitor and remove content deemed “harmful,” even if it’s legal. The goal is to protect users, especially minors, but critics warn it risks mass censorship, harming free speech and everyday conversations about harmless topics like trains or video games.

A petition to repeal the act has gained nearly 480,000 signatures, forcing a parliamentary debate.

If you are a UK citizen, support the petition here: Read the petition

Technology Secretary Peter Kyle said, and I quote, that those who wanted to overturn the act were “on the side of predators.” This claim is obviously false and misleading.

There is also a problem with the fact that this law will be used as a way for companies and the government to collect your biometric and personal data (echem... Google, yeah they love your data) under the excuse “we're protecting the kids.”

I’m not sure if governments think we’re idiots, but I hope this doesn’t get any further than it already has and affect other countries. If everyone complains, we might actually be able to do something.
